About Me

I am a data scientist, AI engineer, and educator whose work sits at the intersection of applied mathematics, machine learning, and meaningful human impact. My path began in pure mathematics and statistics, evolving through enterprise data engineering, full-stack development, and ultimately into AI research and instruction.

My MSc research at the University of Sussex — awarded with Distinction — focused on predicting Alzheimer's disease progression using stochastic modeling and machine learning on large-scale clinical datasets, recognized for its innovative mathematical and data-driven approach.

As an educator, I have guided hundreds of learners across programs at LevelUp Economy, Correlation One, SHAI For AI, and the University of Sussex — empowering professionals to build with confidence in data and AI. Teaching is not separate from my technical work; it is an extension of it.
